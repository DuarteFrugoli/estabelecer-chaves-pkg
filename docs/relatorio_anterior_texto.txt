Abstract—The exponential advancement of wireless communi-
cation technologies has brought numerous benefits across various
sectors, enabling greater connectivity and automation. However,
this progress has also heightened concerns regarding information
security, as the expanded attack surface introduces new vulne-
rabilities and potential cyber threats. To mitigate these risks,
establishing secure channels for cryptographic key exchange is
essential to ensure the confidentiality of communications. This
study investigates a key establishment method based on the
physical properties of the wireless channel, using channel gain
as a secret. This approach stands out for not requiring high
computational power or excessive energy consumption, making
it a viable alternative for next-generation networks such as B5G
and IoT. Two reconciliation methods were analyzed, considering
different error-correcting codes and propagation scenarios. The
results demonstrate that key sequence length directly impacts the
success rate of reconciliation, with longer sequences, combined
with advanced coding techniques, maximizing security and ef-
ficiency. Thus, this study reinforces the feasibility of using the
physical channel as a key generation mechanism, contributing to
enhanced security in emerging wireless communications.
